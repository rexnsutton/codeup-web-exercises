FAQs about National Parks

How Many National Parks are in America?
Today, there are 58 national parks in the United States.
What state has most national parks?
California
Which is the smallest national park in the US?
Hot Springs National Park, Arkansas
What is the oldest National park in the US?
Yellowstone National Park
What President created the most national parks?
President Theodore Roosevelt
What is the newest national park?
On the shores of Lake Michigan is the newest national park in the U.S., the Indiana Dunes, authorized by Congress as a national lakeshore in 1966 and upgraded to national park status on Feb. 15, 2019.
What is the most visited national park?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park is the nation's most popular national park.
What is the only state that does not have a national park?
Delaware
Which National Park makes the most money?
Acadia National Park, 3.5 million.
How do national parks get funded?

Established by Congress 54 years ago, the fund takes revenues from the royalties earned by offshore oil and gas operations and uses them to pay for the preservation and upkeep of public lands and waters in all 50 states.

Yosemite National Park

  • Yosemite’s granite rock formations glow like fire at sunset.
  • The park’s diverse landscape supports more than 400 species.
  • Yosemite is home to one of the tallest waterfalls in the world.
  • Yosemite is the only national park to bid to host the winter Olympics.

YellowStone National Park

  • Yellowstone National Park was established on March 1, 1872.
  • Yellowstone is the world’s first national park.
  • Yellowstone encompasses 3,472 square miles (2,221,766 acres) which makes it larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined
  • Yellowstone is home to more than 500 active geysers (more than half the world's geysers)

Zion National Park

  • The park was established on November 19, 1919
  • It covers an area of 593 square kilometers (229 square miles).
  • The lowest elevation is 1,117 meters (3,666 feet)
  • There is a subway in Zion
